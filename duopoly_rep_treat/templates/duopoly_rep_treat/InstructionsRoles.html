This is an experiment studying behavior in a simple game called a “market”. In this game you will play the role of either a “buyer” or a “seller”.

If you are a seller, you earn tokens by producing objects and selling them to buyers.

If you a buyer, you earn tokens by buying objects from sellers and then giving them to the experimenter in exchange for tokens.

You will play this game many times. Each time you play the game, we call this a “round”.

Each round, you will be grouped with 3 other participants. Two of you will be sellers. The other two will be buyers.

Who is in your group and what role you play are determined randomly by the computer at the start of each round.

Each round works like this:

  1. The two sellers set the prices (in tokens) of their objects
  2. The two buyers see the sellers’ prices
  3. Each buyer chooses a seller they want to buy from
  4. Each seller produces the number of objects that they sold
  5. Each buyer pays the price of the object they bought to the appropriate seller
  6. Buyers give the object they bought to the experimenter in exchange for tokens
